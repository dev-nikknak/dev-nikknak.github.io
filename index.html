Wild Yonder

Unity, C#

My work on Wild Yonder began back in 2020. We (Players Attic) had an idea of the type of story we'd like to tell and through many evolutions of genre and type of game we landed on what kind of game to make. A seafararing adventure set in a narratively rich world, all topped off with a beautiful stylised and vibrant art style. I began prototyping player and scene mechanics in a makeshift test environment and in 2022, began the production of a proof of concept demo. Since then we have showcased at GDLX in March 2023 and are internally debating the next steps of the journey.

So what did I do?

I helped conceptualised the game's core mechanics and then set about implementing them into the demo. As the sole programmer on the team at the time it was also my responsibilty to implement all assets, timeline sequences, animations, sounds, and visual effects. Throughout the process of implementing each new feature or making any significant change I playtested and fixed any bugs.
